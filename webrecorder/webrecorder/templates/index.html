Our Mission

Web Archiving for All!

Webrecorder is a web archiving service anyone can use for free to save web pages. Making a capture is as easy as browsing a page like you normally would. Webrecorder automatically archives the page, along with any additional content triggered by interactions.

This open-source project is brought to you by Rhizome at the New Museum. Development has been generously supported by the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation and the Knight Foundation.

Online Now ≠ Online Forever

Links break. Services disappear and redesigns happen. Make sure that a copy of what’s important to you will stay available.

Your web, your data

Archive items from web sites that require you to log in. Webrecorder does not store credentials.

All the web archives you create are downloadable in the ISO standard WARC file format.

Make your collections publicly accessible or keep them private.

Web Preservation for Web Media

Web sites are more than screenshots: they reveal information in interaction, embed different types of media and link to other resources. The way webrecorder captures web media ensures it stays recallable in the future.

And even if your favorite browser will be obsolete in 5 years: Webrecorder is keeping a running copy of it so behaviours will stay the same.

Offline Viewing

Rhizome provides Webrecorder Player so you can open the exported web archives even when you are offline.
